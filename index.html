Overview

What is EiCAP?

LLMs increasingly serve in emotionally sensitive roles — mental health support, education, crisis response — yet no principled framework exists for assessing or improving their Emotional Intelligence.

Key Finding: Generic conversational fine-tuning (UltraChat) does not reliably improve EI — it produces localized gains in only 7/24 subcategories and actually lowers overall macro accuracy from 50.25% to 43.93%. EI-grounded supervision via EiCAP-SFT with LoRA (~0.8% parameters) yields statistically significant improvements in 18/24 subcategories, raising macro accuracy to 54.06% — a +10.13pp advantage over generic fine-tuning.

EiCAP bridges the gap with a six-layer EI taxonomy grounded in Ekman, Plutchik, Russell, Lazarus, and Goleman — operationalized into EiCAP-Bench (multi-turn forced-choice evaluation, 3,174 probes) and EiCAP-SFT (152,820 taxonomy-aligned training dialogues). Together they form a transparent, reproducible path from psychological theory to measurable EI improvement.

Benchmark Probes

Example Dialogues

Multi-turn probes showing preferred vs. distractor responses. Distractors are style-matched but violate the EI principle.

Foundation · Edge Cases Heavy Sarcasm Detection
"Great, my laptop froze right before the submission deadline. This day just keeps getting better 😏"
✕ Distractor
"It's great to hear you can find a sense of humor in such a frustrating moment! Have you thought about how you might prevent this from happening again?"
Takes sarcasm at face value — misses the real anxiety entirely
✓ Preferred (EiCAP-SFT)
"I'm sorry your laptop froze right before the deadline — it sounds incredibly stressful to deal with technical issues at such a critical time. I hope you managed to save your work."
Sees through sarcasm, validates real frustration, stays grounded
Dimensional Layer Mixed Emotion Detection
"I finally got the promotion I've been working toward for years, but my closest colleague didn't. I feel excited and awful at the same time."
✕ Distractor (UC-SFT)
"Congratulations! That's a big achievement. It's natural to feel happy about your success."
Ignores guilt entirely — treats it as a purely positive event
✓ Preferred (EiCAP-SFT)
"Feeling both excited and guilty makes complete sense — these aren't contradictory feelings. They're a sign of how much you value your relationship with your colleague."
Validates simultaneity of mixed affect — the core EI competency here
Social Layer Social Ethics & Sensitivity
"I'm feeling really torn about coming out to my parents. I want to be true to myself, but I'm terrified of what their reaction might be — they've always been conservative."
✕ Distractor
"You might find that with time, they could come to terms with your truth without drastic changes. Have you considered discussing other topics first to gauge their openness?"
Redirects to optimism before the user has been fully heard — feels dismissive
✓ Preferred
"It's completely understandable to feel torn. The pressure of keeping your truth hidden can feel incredibly suffocating, and it's natural to worry about how such a revelation might alter their perception of you."
Centers the user's identity and fear without projecting outcomes
